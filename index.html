Tommy Schneider Tutoring

Seattle/Redmond Area Tutoring

Bio

My name is Tommy Schneider. I have a Bachelor of Science from Harvey Mudd College where I studied math, physics, and computer science. I have five years of tutoring experience at the college and high-school level. In addition to teaching, my passions include playing chess and writing comedic fiction. Right now, I am working part-time at the Redmond Mathnasium and am saving money to obtain a teaching certification in Washington State.

Approach

My approach to teaching is compassionate and flexible. I firmly believe mistakes are a necessary part of the learning process. Having the chance to explore new and unfamiliar ideas alongside an experienced instructor is one of the best ways to learn and achieve long-lasting success. In addition to helping students with particular exams and assignments, I work to identify and fill knowledge gaps and help the student develop a general approach to technical problem solving.

Subjects

I have a strong background in mathematics, and can teach Algebra I, Geometry, Algebra II, Precalculus, Calculus, Multivariable Calculus, Linear Algebra, Real Analysis, Abstract Algebra, and much more! I’ve also tutored college-level physics courses, including Classical Mechanics and Electricity and Magnetism.

Pricing

My pricing generally depends on circumstance (how much time I need to spend preparing, commute, etc.), but is generally in the $30-$50/hour range. However, the first lesson is always free. The best way to see if we’d be a good fit is to setup a session and see!
